# Ernest Strauhal

**Ernest Charles Strauhal · 許志仁 — born 8:30am, October 15, 2000, Children's Hospital, San Francisco. The person the whole vault is a map of.** The youngest and oldest child of four: three miscarriages came before him, so he arrived as both the first to survive and the last they had.

## The shape of it
Chinese-American, raised on the move: the family left [[san francisco|San Francisco]]
for Sacramento in 2003. School ran from Elliot Ranch Elementary through West Campus High;
piano started in 2008. He entered the design program at CCA (formerly CCAC) in 2018 and left it in
2019 — the first of the [[Failures and Abandoned Projects|abandonments]]. Graduate school
at SAIC followed in 2022, then a year of [[The Record Label|shows and releases]] in New
York and Chicago in 2023, then the 2024 collapse — no work, his aunt and uncle's basement
in suburban Illinois, antidepressants, and the start of the [[diary]]. 2025 was the bottom
and the turn: failure in Chicago, the return to California, suicidal ideation, and then
redemption — a job teaching visual art to children on digital whiteboards. In 2026 he
[[Megan|proposed]] and took a conservatory job playing and teaching piano to
mentally and physically handicapped children and adults. The full sequence lives in
[[Map - Timeline]].

## What it explains
- **The teaching lineage.** His father taught high-school math; his mother taught a
  Chinese-English first-grade immersion program. He now teaches art and piano to children
  — the sketchbook's "to truly educate is to render myself unnecessary" is inherited.
- **[[Latent Space]] as autobiography.** When he imagines the self as "vector coordinates"
  of "nationality, language, race," he is describing his own coordinates: Guangzhou and
  Santa Cruz, Cantonese and English, [[grandmas house|Chinatown]] and the Sacramento suburbs.
- **[[Autofiction]].** The diary that narrativizes his life is the same impulse as this node.
