# How has my view of AI authorship and control changed?

Your view has shifted across several areas concerning the nature of creation, authorship, and technological mediation:

*   **Authorship and Control in AI Art:** Your engagement with AI art involves a visible negotiation between human input, algorithmic output, and final editorial judgment [[knowledge/wiki/sources/No More Human Art|No More Human Art - Source Essay]]. In processes like the "Human-Machine Tug of War," you establish source material, but the machine generates additions that cannot be fully predicted; ultimately, your aesthetic judgment determines the finished work [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/Human-Machine Tug of War|Human-Machine Tug of War]], [[knowledge/wiki/sources/No More Human Art|No More Human Art - Source Essay]]. While you use tools like StyleGAN and Pix2Pix yourself, you acknowledge that even your own "self-aware" work can be funneled through algorithms outside of your control [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/AI Slop|AI Slop]].
*   **The Self as Data:** Your concept of self extends into technical spaces. You view identity and feeling as trajectories within a continuous interpolated space, the "latent space," which is central to understanding how personal data can be modeled or continued by AI [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/Latent Space|Latent Space]]. This was exemplified in works like *Vocal Study 3*, where algorithmic continuation of your voice and body resulted in an uncanny doppelganger, representing a negotiation over who or what might continue you [[knowledge/wiki/works/Vocal Study 3|Vocal Study 3]], [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/Latent Space|Latent Space]].
*   **The Medium as Self-Reflection:** Your artistic practices have become increasingly self-referential regarding technological mediation. For instance, in *Video Portraiture*, the process involves a loop where a neural network makes aesthetic choices, but your hand must re-integrate and color-correct the output [[knowledge/wiki/works/Video Portraiture|Video Portraiture]]. Furthermore, you recognize that even private acts of preservation, like creating an archive or diary, are shaped for an audience, leading to anxieties about control over one's own image once it is digitized (e.g., your voice model) [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/The Internet as Confidant|The Internet as Confidant], [knowledge/wiki/works/kits.ai Voice|kits.ai Voice]].
*   **Narrative Structure:** You have moved away from treating the diary as a pure record of events, recognizing that it has morphed into "autofiction," meaning you are actively narrativizing your life within it [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/Autofiction|Autofiction]]. This realization led to the decision that your future artworks would function as your diary entries, moving the act of self-narration back into the art itself [[knowledge/wiki/concepts/Autofiction|Autofiction]].

Open Questions
Evidence is incomplete regarding how this change in view affects your *future* artistic direction beyond stating that compositions will replace diary entries.
