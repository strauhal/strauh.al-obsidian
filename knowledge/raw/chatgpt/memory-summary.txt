Overview
You are an artist, musician, and teacher whose work moves between drawing, music, writing, philosophy, and technology. Much of your recent activity centers on teaching visual art and piano, developing a broad creative practice that treats drawing as a foundational way of thinking, and exploring music theory, composition, and piano technique in depth. You frequently engage with philosophy, aesthetics, cultural history, and creative tools, and you tend to prefer detailed, rigorous explanations over simplified summaries.

Creative Practice
Your creative work spans drawing, painting, photography, music, writing, and digital media. You work with materials ranging from watercolor, gouache, acrylic paint pens, Rapidograph pens, Polaroids, and Instax film to software such as Ableton, Premiere Pro, and Pages. You maintain an art archive at strauh.al/art and operate computerdrawing.club, an anonymous drawing platform. A recurring theme in your work is treating drawing as a foundational cognitive act that can connect image, sound, text, and time-based media into larger integrated works. You have been developing a book of diary entries and notebook drawings covering roughly 2024–2026, and you often think about how visual art, music, philosophy, and personal documentation can be combined into a single practice.

Teaching and Work
You currently teach and perform in creative fields. Your recent work includes teaching visual art to children using digital whiteboards and projection systems, as well as playing and teaching piano for handicapped children and adults. You have also prepared application materials for a Studio Art/Drawing Lecturer position at California State University, Sacramento. Your proposed course, "Drawing as Total Practice," presents drawing as a foundational activity extending across images, music, writing, video, and digital tools rather than as a narrow technical skill. You frequently communicate with students and families in both English and Spanish and have handled scheduling, lesson planning, and educational administration.

Music and Piano
Music is a major ongoing focus. You study piano, music theory, composition, improvisation, sight-reading, and musical memory, and you often look for the most effective ways to improve quickly. Topics you have explored include modes, melodic minor, chromaticism, tonic and dominant relationships, pedal tones, rhythm, harmonic analysis, and compositional techniques used by composers such as Chopin and John Adams. You are interested in both historical and contemporary music, with influences ranging from Chopin, Scriabin, Terry Riley, and Ryuichi Sakamoto to Arca, King Crimson, Massive Attack, and Playboi Carti. You use a 37-key MIDI keyboard and have discussed audio technology such as lossless playback with AirPods Max.

Philosophy and Reading
You regularly engage with philosophy, literary culture, and intellectual history. Recent interests include Heidegger, Hegel, Husserl, Kierkegaard, Nietzsche, phenomenology, continental philosophy, psychoanalysis, media theory, and questions about technology and modernity. Your reading interests span literature, philosophy, art criticism, cultural theory, comics, memoir, and experimental writing. Books and authors associated with your interests include Hegel, Camus, Baudelaire, David Foster Wallace, Osamu Dazai, Nick Land, Bruno Munari, Neil Postman, Khalil Gibran, Richard Powers, Junji Ito, and many others. You tend to approach these subjects through detailed questions rather than broad overviews and often connect philosophical ideas back to art, music, and everyday life.

Background
You were born in San Francisco in 2000 and moved with your family to the Sacramento area during childhood. You attended Elliot Ranch Elementary, Elizabeth Pinkerton Middle School, and West Campus High School. You were accepted to the California College of the Arts design program in 2018 and later to the School of the Art Institute of Chicago graduate program in 2022. You have exhibited work in New York and Chicago and have been involved in music performance and releases. You have Chinese ancestry through family members who came to San Francisco during the railroad era, and you have shown interest in the cultural history surrounding that heritage.

Creative Influences
Your influences span visual art, music, literature, design, and fashion. Artists, musicians, and thinkers connected to your interests include Joseph Beuys, Egon Schiele, M. C. Escher, Barry McGee, Clyfford Still, John Singer Sargent, Rei Kawakubo, Jesse Kanda, Scriabin, Chopin, Terry Riley, Arca, Dean Blunt, King Crimson, Massive Attack, Ryuichi Sakamoto, Camus, Baudelaire, Proust, and David Foster Wallace. You are especially drawn to ambitious creators whose work crosses disciplinary boundaries, and you often search for artists who function as total practitioners rather than specialists in a single medium.

Tools and Technology
You enjoy exploring creative and technical tools in detail. Topics you have investigated include AI image generation, prompting techniques for large language models, audio formats, e-paper displays, Raspberry Pi hardware, software history, image-model behavior, and digital publishing workflows. You often evaluate tools through the lens of artistic practice rather than pure technical efficiency, asking how software, interfaces, and media can support deeper forms of creative thought. You also tend to seek practical recommendations with specific constraints rather than generic product advice.
