# The Flâneur as Web Surfer

**The 19th-century stroller of the arcades, rebooted as the aimless drifter of the feed — the figure at the heart of the [[Arcades Project (Revisited)]].**

## Summary
Benjamin built his arcades around the *flâneur*, the idle, hyper-observant walker who
reads the crowd and the shop windows. The [[arcades project|Arcades Project]] rewrites
that figure directly: the websites are "vast virtual corridors," and the user is the
flâneur who wanders them "during times of boredom or curiosity." It is the human posture
[[The Attention Economy]] is designed to capture and the [[The Internet as Confidant|net]]
is designed to receive — and a cousin to the rail-rider of [[Trainpilled]], another kind
of drift through a built landscape.

## Key points
- Wandering as a mode of attention — receptive, unhurried, the opposite of the feed's pull.
- [[Charles Baudelaire]] → Benjamin → Ernest: the lineage the work makes explicit.
- The arcade and the browser are the same architecture: corridors lined with things to want.
