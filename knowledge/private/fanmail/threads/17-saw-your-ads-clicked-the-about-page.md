## 2025-08-19 - Correspondent 17

**Direction:** incoming

Wild how influential /lit/ was (and continues to be) for like... what, 5k people max in the US? Whenever I think about /lit/ or /mu/ I think about that Velvet Underground quip that's like "6 people heard that record but every single one started a band" or something. Occasionally I'll see someone's shelf or hear someone online talk about books and I'll realize that they were also a /lit/ poster. Was very influential for me personally from 2012-15 or so. Will never forget opening a thread for the first time where someone asked "what's the final boss of /lit/" and someone posted a jpg of Finnegans Wake and I still remember thinking "who the hell is james joyce?" lmao

Thx for the diary link. Reminds me of Meg Boyle's thing from the alt-lit scene way back when. Agreed re; liking Rembrandt's sketches more than his paintings -- I don't know a ton about art, but I'm always way more drawn to an artist's notebooks, sketchbooks, drafts etc than I am their finished pieces. Something about how the notebook is raw creative force made tangible and the finished piece is a posture -- which isn't necessarily bad of course, but it's a different kind of energy. I remember seeing a .jpg of Picasso's poetry notebooks where he just daubed paint mid-sentence instead of finding the right color word. That kinda stuff you know? Happy to hear about a sequence of most wonderful events that reignited your will to live.

Re; MFA and art; would love any recommendations you have for contemporary/modern shit that you enjoy, especially artists who you think are doing something new, interesting, exciting. Always trying to expand my taste.

Disintegration Loops is beautiful. I used to throw it on and take really long walks around town when I was in college. One of my all time favs.

Love your website btw. Drawn to the repository aspect, kinda like a 21st c. dymaxion chronofile thing. I think I'm going to steal your idea and make my own website. How did you do yours? I know literally nothing about web design other than "I've seen a preroll ad for Squarespace." Been kicking around a few folders on my laptop/pgs in some notebooks that I've been meaning to actually do something with.

Best,
