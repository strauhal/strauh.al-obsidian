# The Oedipal Screen

**Ernest's reading of his pull toward VR and the computer through an Oedipal frame: the screen as the supreme power that has displaced both father and mother.**

## Summary
Diary, 2024-11-22: "why am i so drawn towards the vr headset? could it be that i'm
trying to grasp towards the source of power in the oedipal love triangle?… now both the
father and the mother have succumbed to a greater power: the screen." He dates the draw
to a primal scene — "the first time i saw the blue glow of the computer screen against
my father's face… as a 5 year old, i intuitively knew there was a kind of sacred energy
about the computer" — and begging to be let onto his father's lap to "experience the
internet for the first time." VR is "the most visceral, uncompromising exposure to that
oedipal power. like riding a missile through cyberspace."

## Key points
- The wish to escape the body entirely: "get rid of my body and just keep my brain
  hooked up to a computer" (2024-08-28).
- VR + Wellbutrin produce strange hybrid dreams where real people and avatars coexist.
- He is self-aware that this is "cringe and bluepilled" — register slides toward [[Post-Irony]].
