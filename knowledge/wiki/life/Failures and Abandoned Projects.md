# Failures and Abandoned Projects

> "part of the fun when my life falls apart is that it gets even better when i rebuild it." — [[ideas sketchbook 2020 25|sketchbook]]

**A deliberate ledger of the things that stopped — what they were, why they ended, and what they produced anyway. Patterns in failure are more instructive than patterns in success.**

## The record label
An attempt to start a label; it got as far as a webpage and a Bandcamp page before he
quit (diary, 2024-09-13). The stated reason became a whole idea: "how can artists compete
with an endless stream of entertainment tailored to the user?" → see [[The Record Label]]
and [[The Attention Economy]].
**Produced anyway:** the clearest articulation of why the feed defeats sustained work.

## The slop period (2022 – early 2023)
By his own account "my slop period… truly artistically bankrupt" — stretching 15-second
snippets of obscure songs into six-minute pieces. It ended when [[Ryuichi Sakamoto]] died
and he "realized i needed to put more effort into my music."
**Produced anyway:** the negative example that defined [[AI Slop]] and reset the bar for
[[The Tedium of the Art Is the Goal]].

## The MIT application
He sent an application to MIT's graduate architecture program (diary), already braced for
the rejection he expected in the spring.
**Produced anyway:** the months of waiting became the occasion to finally read Hegel's
*Phenomenology of Spirit*.

## The 2024–2025 bottom — and the turn
The largest failure was not a project but a place. Unable to find work in Chicago in 2024,
[[Ernest Strauhal|Ernest]] moved into his aunt and uncle's basement in suburban Illinois,
dependent on antidepressants during a grab-bag psychiatric diagnosis — a flat, shut-down
stretch where, as he put it, the bad thoughts went quiet and "all my other thoughts go
away too." The [[diary]] starts here. 2025 was the floor: the failure in Chicago, the
return to California, suicidal ideation.
**Produced anyway — the redemption.** It turned. He found work teaching visual art to
children on digital whiteboards, then in 2026 a music-conservatory job teaching piano to
handicapped children and adults, and he [[Megan|proposed]]. The bottom is what the rest of
the vault is measured against — the literal content of [[Amor Fati]], learning to rebuild
from it, and the baseline the [[The Ceremony|ceremony]] is meant to work on.
