# Arcades Project (Revisited)

**Ernest's web work modeled on Walter Benjamin's [[The Arcades Project|Arcades Project]]: a montage of quotes, screenshots, and fragments filed into lettered "convolutes," but with the 19th-century Paris arcade swapped for the internet.**

## Summary
The [[arcades project|page]] opens by re-writing Benjamin's famous description of the
arcades as a description of *websites* — "vast virtual corridors extending through the
landscape of the internet… a world in miniature, in which users will find everything
they need." It keeps Benjamin's method intact: lettered convolutes (a = the digital
arcades and "web surfers," b = fashion, d = boredom and eternal return), each a collage
of found text — tweets, greentext, AI-slop captions — indexed in his hand like `[A1,1]`.
It is the vault's clearest piece of *criticism-as-art*: a structure borrowed wholesale
from a dead theorist and turned on the present.

## Why it matters
- It is the bridge from the reading library into the works: the [[The Arcades Project]]
  (Benjamin) is in the shelves; this is what Ernest *made* of it.
- It makes [[The Attention Economy]] and [[The Internet as Confidant]] into a *form*,
  not just themes — the feed rendered as a 19th-century promenade.
