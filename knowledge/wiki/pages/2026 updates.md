# [strauh.al](https://strauh.al)/2026_updates include:

 a major overhaul to how the artworks are currently categorized;
 names like "[fine_art](https://strauh.al/fine_art)" "[art_misc](https://strauh.al/art_misc)" "[aesthetics](https://strauh.al/aesthetics)" "[2025_art](https://strauh.al/2025_art)" "[more_fine_art](https://strauh.al/more_fine_art)" and "[somehow_even_more_art](https://strauh.al/somehow_even_more_art)" have been phased out for directory names pertaining to the century in which the artwork was made and have been consolidated on the new [art](https://strauh.al/art) page.

 each artwork now has a proper title instead of a string of random numbers and characters.

 this is in service of adding an interactive 3D museum interface to the archive.

[here is a link to a mockup](https://strauh.al/museum/mockup)

 when mixed reality glasses become mainstream in a decade, the archive will be fully ready to be deployed in xr format.

 i have also decided to add a "randomize order" button to the [art](https://strauh.al/art) page to keep the spirit of discovery and anachronism alive in response to this nice letter:

 Dear Webmaster,

 I can say this is the coolest website out there. I can't say I like the art being arranged in linear fashion. Links like Art, More Art and Even more fine art were some of my favorites. I thought it was a nice juxtaposition to see some art from 1654 next to some fabulous sci-fi peice from 1970.

 Anyway that's the two cents of someone on the internet.

 Thank you kindly,

 Raul

 thank you all for your continued support and have a nice 2026

 best,
 ernest
