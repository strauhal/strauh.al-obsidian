# Reading Like a Computer

**The attention damage of the feed, felt at the level of reading itself: scanning for the point instead of inhabiting the text.**

## Summary
In the [[Albert Camus|Camus]] thread, Ernest admits he has "lost the ability to read
fiction" and "anxiously wait[s] for camus to 'arrive at the point.'" He generalizes it
(2024-12-29): "we just scan texts like computers instead of engaging with them as humans."
It is [[The Attention Economy]] turned inward — the specific cognitive cost behind the
[[Map - Library|library]] he owns but can't always read, and the human side of the same
machine logic he critiques as [[AI Slop]].

## Key points
- Comprehension degraded into extraction — reading as ctrl-F.
- The library as evidence: hundreds of ebooks, a shrinking capacity to sit with one.
- Why the [[Spontaneity and Elegance|slow, human]] act of reading becomes a deliberate counter-practice.
