# Family

**The lineage behind [[Ernest Strauhal|Ernest]] — two sides that meet in San Francisco, and that quietly seed half the vault's recurring themes.**

## Mother's side — Guangzhou to Chinatown
- **Mother:** born in Guangzhou, China; moved to San Francisco's [[grandmas house|Chinatown]] at 11. She taught a Chinese–English first-grade immersion program — one root of the [[Ernest Strauhal|teaching lineage]], and of [[Latent Space]] read as autobiography (language and nationality as coordinates of the self).
- **Maternal grandfather:** a day trader who listened to the radio each morning and carried his abacus to the Hong Kong stock exchange to buy and sell shares — a life lived by the morning information stream, a strange ancestor to [[The Attention Economy]].
- **Maternal great-(great-)grandfather:** the details are murky, but he helped build the railroads in California and gained citizenship after the **1906 San Francisco great fire**. He is the literal, ancestral root of [[Trainpilled]] — the rail affinity was in the blood before it was an aesthetic.

## Father's side — Santa Cruz and the wars
- **Father:** born in Santa Cruz, California; a high-school math teacher; married Ernest's mother in San Francisco in 1993. The other root of the teaching lineage.
- **Paternal grandfather:** fought in the **Battle of the Bulge** in World War II.
- **Paternal great-grandfather:** an ambulance driver in France and Belgium in World War I.

## Why it's in the vault
A vault that maps a consciousness has to hold the people who shaped it. These aren't
influences in the [[Map - Influences|art-historical]] sense — they're the substrate. The
railroad and the immersion classroom and the two European wars are all upstream of the
work, and now they have somewhere to connect to.
