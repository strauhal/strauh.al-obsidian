# The Internet as Confidant

**Ernest's recurring sense that the internet is a confidant, a consciousness, even a sacred presence — the entity his diary and archive are addressed to.**

## Summary
Writing the diary in raw HTML (2024-09-10): "right now, it just feels like me and the
internet… if the infrastructure powering the internet developed a consciousness, this
diary would be a nice little secret that me and the internet would share." Later
(2024-11-25): "there is something quite spiritual about using the internet… bits of your
mind… leaving through your fingers traveling at near light speed around the globe." The
[[strauh.al Archive]] is the fullest expression — "the closest thing to my consciousness
that i can capture in html."

## Key points
- Validation paradox (2025-01-10): even a diary, the most private form, is shaped for an
  audience of "a small number of people [who] can visit these thoughts anytime."
- Accountability (2025-01-15): semi-public writing keeps him "in check" — a safeguard, after
  others described an early version as "a really long suicide note."
- The first sight of "the blue glow of the computer screen against my father's face"
  reads as origin myth (see [[The Oedipal Screen]]).
