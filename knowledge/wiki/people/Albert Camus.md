# Albert Camus

**French-Algerian writer and philosopher of the absurd; an influence Ernest reads even as he wrestles with his own attention span.**

## Summary
Listed among the [[influences]]. The diary (2024-09-29) is candid about the difficulty:
"i feel like i've lost the ability to read fiction. i anxiously wait for camus to 'arrive
at the point.'" He recounts mistaking *The Plague* for a book "about rats" until an
advisor told him it was about Nazism. He later generalizes it (2024-12-29) into a thesis
about [[AI Slop]]-era reading: "we just scan texts like computers instead of engaging
with them as humans."

- [[AI Slop]] — the "reading like a computer" worry.
