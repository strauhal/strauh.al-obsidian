# Evolution of Ernest's Thinking

Here is a chronological trace of changes, contradictions, and recurring returns in the artist's thinking, organized by theme.

***

## Thematic Analysis of Artistic Thinking

### 🧠 AI and Authorship / Artifice vs. Authenticity
The artist shows a deep preoccupation with what constitutes "real" creation, often contrasting human effort with digital mimicry or external influence.

*   **Contradiction/Shift:** Initially dismissive of commercialized art forms (Sept 13th), the artist later critiques the *process* of sharing art online (Sept 23rd).
*   **Evidence:** The critique of social media activism, noting that "people are just arguing over ai generated activism slop" and that the performance is for "optics now" (Oct 20th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Recurring Return:** A recurring skepticism regarding digital authenticity. The artist notes how easily AI can generate convincing, yet hollow, content (e.g., the viral "All Eyes on Rafah" image) (Oct 20th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Inference:** The artist views AI-generated art as a symptom of cultural decay—a race to the bottom where performance replaces genuine effort.

### 🎨 Artistic Control and Process
The discussion around making art shifts from self-doubt regarding creative capacity to critiques of modern artistic presentation.

*   **Change/Loss:** A stark realization that medication might suppress creativity (Aug 31st). The artist states, "well, i no longer want to make art anymore. any creative impulses... have now been beaten out" (Aug 31st) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Contradiction:** Despite this loss of impulse, the artist continues to document artistic critiques and plans for future work (e.g., "video portraiture," Oct 24th; or planning to make the website an "ark for human made art," Ideas Sketchbook) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Recurring Return:** A fixation on *immediacy* and *spontaneity*. The artist praises spontaneous sketches over labored technique (Oct 25th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], contrasting this with the "self discipline to crank out a work of art in one sitting, in front of a camera" (Sept 23rd) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Evidence:** The artist notes that their current practice is documenting *the self* via HTML rather than traditional media ("I’m just left baring everything in HTML. It’s ugly.") (Nov 7th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].

### 🌐 Internet Identity and Preservation
The medium of the diary itself becomes a subject of contemplation, linking personal documentation to digital permanence.

*   **Change:** The artist moves from thinking about physical journals to realizing their current format is inherently digital and public-facing.
*   **Evidence:** "i'm writing this in html that will be hosted on a url for others to read if they have the link... it just feels like me and the internet" (Sept 10th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Recurring Return:** The concept of the diary as an archive or "ark." This is explicitly stated in the Ideas Sketchbook ("this website will now be an ark for human made art") and implied by the nature of hosting it online (Sept 10th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].

### ⚕️ Mortality, Illness, and Control
Mortality appears as a recurring undercurrent, often linked to physical decline or medical intervention.

*   **Evidence:** Direct expressions of suicidal ideation ("i wish i were dead," Aug 31st) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]]. Later, the desire for a quick death is stated when discussing illness (Oct 13th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Contradiction:** The artist oscillates between profound despair and an almost detached intellectualization of decline. They are morbidly interested in how others face mortality, such as the discussion around ALS and feeding tubes (Oct 16th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Inference:** There is a pattern of using external stressors—medication side effects, family illness, or existential dread—as catalysts for intense self-reflection.

### ❤️ Love, Connection, and Relationships
Connections are viewed through lenses of shared experience, cultural critique, or necessary distance.

*   **Evidence (Positive):** Positive moments involve connection with others outside the immediate circle: meeting an "online friend" (Oct 7th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], or memories with family like visiting a cafe with their father (Sept 24th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Evidence (Critical):** The artist is critical of societal expectations placed on relationships, such as the pressure to be "content" from their mother (Nov 3rd) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].

### ✨ Spirituality and Philosophy
The thinking moves between grand philosophical systems and cynical deconstruction.

*   **Recurring Return:** Philosophical frameworks are used both for aspiration and for mockery. The artist invokes *Amor fati* (Sept 4th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], suggesting a desire to embrace fate, but this is often undercut by cynicism regarding modern life's superficiality.
*   **Evidence:** A deep dive into philosophical concepts like "latent space" and vector coordinates for human identity (Sept 17th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], suggesting a desire to map and understand the totality of human experience rationally.
*   **Contradiction:** The high-minded intellectualism (Latent Space, *Amor fati*) frequently collapses into petty observations about social performance or cultural trends (e.g., "the best answer... is 'a tribe called quest'" on Sept 27th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].

### 📣 Attention and Boredom
The management of attention—both receiving it and giving it—is a primary driver of the entries.

*   **Initial State:** Extreme boredom drives the entire project ("today i have become so bored that i have decided to start a diary") (Aug 27th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Shift:** The artist becomes acutely aware of how attention is commodified and manipulated by technology. This leads to critiques of short-form content (Sept 13th) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], and the observation that everyone "doomscrolls on the same apps" (Oct 1st) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]].
*   **Recurring Return:** The desire to *control* attention. This is seen in the critique of performance art ("people sharing timelapses... it just feels..weird?") (Sept 23rd) [Source: [[knowledge/raw/diary|diary]]], and the later assertion that their own diary writing, though "shallow navel gazing," is a deliberate act of self-focus against external noise.

***
### Summary of Key Shifts & Contradictions

1.  **From Internal Struggle to External Critique:** The initial entries are dominated by personal chemical/mental distress (Wellbutrin, boredom) [Aug 27th - Aug 31st]. This evolves into a sophisticated critique of *culture* itself—critiquing social media activism, art presentation, and cultural trends (Oct 20th onwards).
2.  **From Physical to Digital Medium:** The physical act of writing in a journal gives way to the self-aware documentation of being "in html" for the internet (Sept 10th), making the *platform* part of the subject matter.
3.  **The Role of Effort:** There is a tension between valuing raw, spontaneous genius (the sketch, the immediate feeling) and recognizing that modern culture rewards highly polished, easily digestible, or algorithmically optimized content (Reels, AI art).
