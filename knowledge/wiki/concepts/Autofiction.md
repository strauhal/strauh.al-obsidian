# Autofiction

**Ernest's own term for what the diary became: "this diary began as an earnest (heh) attempt at recording daily events, but has morphed into autofiction. i am actively narrativizing in my life. don't believe half the things you read in this diary."**

## Summary
By 2024-11-28 he no longer trusts the diary as record. He notes that everyone on social
media "spoonfeeds you a narrativized version of their life," but typing it out and
reading it back makes the narrativization conscious and "a bit more mental." He prunes
entries that "did not sound like they were written by a mentally well individual"
(2024-12-28), and admits the diary's direction is shaped by the knowledge that others
might read it (see [[The Internet as Confidant]]).

## Key points
- Sincerity is unstable: an angry early entry gets annotated — "i can't remember if this
  was meant to be sincere, ironic, or post-ironic" (links to [[Post-Irony]]).
- The final entry retires the diary: "my artworks and compositions will function as my
  diary entries from this point" — narrativization moves back into the art.

## Connections
- [[Post-Irony]] — the unstable register the narrativizing voice slides between.
- [[The Internet as Confidant]] — the audience that bends the narrative.
- [[Memory and Preservation]] — narration as another way of keeping the past.
