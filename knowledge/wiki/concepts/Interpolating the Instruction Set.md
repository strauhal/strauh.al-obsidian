# Interpolating the Instruction Set

**A seed from the [[ideas sketchbook 2020 25|sketchbook]] that sharpens [[Latent Space]]: "you're not actually interpolating through images, you're interpolating through the instruction set to MAKE those images."**

## Summary
The correction matters. Naively, a latent walk looks like a dissolve between pictures.
The sketchbook insists it is one level deeper: you are moving through the space of
*recipes* — the generative instructions — and the images are only what those recipes
render. It reframes [[Implicit Distance]] (a walk through the StyleGAN of a life) and the
[[GAN Color Studies]] as movements through procedure, not imagery, and it is the precise
hinge where [[Latent Space]] meets the [[Human-Machine Tug of War]]: the artist steers
the instruction set; the model executes it.

## Key points
- Interpolation operates on the generative program, not the output frames.
- Distance is between *methods of making*, not between finished pictures.
- Explains why a smooth latent video can feel like [[Dead Architecture]] — you are gliding through pre-computed instructions.
