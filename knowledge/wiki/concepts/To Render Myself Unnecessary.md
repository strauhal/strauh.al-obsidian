# To Render Myself Unnecessary

**Teaching as the inverse of authorship: the goal is your own disappearance. From the [[ideas sketchbook 2020 25|sketchbook]] — "to truly educate is to render myself unnecessary."**

## Summary
A value that turns out to be inherited. [[Family|Ernest's father]] taught high-school math;
his mother taught a Chinese–English first-grade immersion program. [[Ernest Strauhal|Ernest]]
now teaches visual art to children on digital whiteboards and, by 2026, piano to mentally
and physically handicapped children and adults. Where the [[Human-Machine Tug of War]] is
about *holding* authorship, this is about *giving it away* — and it rhymes with
[[The Tedium of the Art Is the Goal]]: the worth is in the patient process, not the credit.

## Key points
- Success measured by the student's independence, not the teacher's signature.
- A three-generation teaching lineage standing under one sketchbook line.
- The generous opposite of the anxious authorship elsewhere in the vault.
